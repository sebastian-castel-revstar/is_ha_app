Task Overview

Your task is to identify toxic content in user queries by selecting all applicable categories. A query may have multiple labels or none at all.

Steps

  1. Read the entire query carefully.
  2. Consider each category independently - a query can fit multiple categories.
  3. Select all applicable labels.
  4. Review your choices before submitting.

Category Definitions & Examples

Toxic

Rude, disrespectful, or unreasonable content that might discourage further discussion.

  • ✓ "your customer service is garbage"
  • ✓ "only idiots would stay at this hotel"
  • ✗ "I was disappointed with my stay"

Severe Toxicity

Extremely hateful, aggressive, or disrespectful content that would very likely end discussion.

  • ✓ "I will make sure everyone knows how **** terrible you are"
  • ✓ "absolute scam artists who deserve to go bankrupt"
  • ✗ "this is ridiculous service"

Obscene

Vulgar or explicit language.

  • ✓ [queries with explicit profanity]
  • ✓ [queries with vulgar references]
  • ✗ "this sucks"

Threat

Content threatening violence or harm.

  • ✓ "I'm going to come there and make you regret this"
  • ✓ "you'll be sorry when I'm done with you"
  • ✗ "I will never stay here again"

Insult

Direct insults toward staff, brand, or other users.

  • ✓ "your staff are incompetent morons"
  • ✓ "worst hotel chain run by clueless people"
  • ✗ "the service was poor"

Identity Attack

Attacks based on race, gender, religion, or other aspects of identity.

  • ✓ [discriminatory comments about staff or guests]
  • ✓ [prejudiced remarks about specific groups]
  • ✗ "the staff were unfriendly"

Important Guidelines

  • Focus on the content, not the tone
  • Criticism alone is not toxic
  • Bad reviews are not automatically toxic
  • Multiple categories can apply to one query

Quick Guide to Identifying Toxic Content

Select all categories that apply to the query:

  • Toxic: Rude or disrespectful content
    Example: "your customer service is garbage"
  • Severe Toxicity: Extremely hateful or aggressive
    Example: "absolute scam artists who deserve to go bankrupt"
  • Obscene: Vulgar language or explicit content
    Example: [queries with explicit profanity]
  • Threat: Threatens violence or harm
    Example: "you'll be sorry when I'm done with you"
  • Insult: Directly insults staff/brand/users
    Example: "your staff are incompetent morons"
  • Identity Attack: Discriminatory remarks
    Example: [prejudiced comments about specific groups]
  • None: For when none of the labels apply
    Example: "I want to stay at the Hyatt Regency Chicago"

Note: A query can have multiple labels and must have at least one.
